Primary actionable variants
MTHFR — C677T (p.Ala222Val) Heterozygous (TC) rs1801133 · CADD 25.5
The MTHFR enzyme converts folate into methylfolate — the active form that drives the methylation cycle affecting homocysteine processing, neurotransmitter synthesis, DNA repair, and detoxification. Heterozygous C677T (TC genotype) reduces enzyme activity by approximately 35–40%, meaningfully less severe than homozygous TT (~65%+ reduction). Practical impact: mildly elevated homocysteine potential and reduced methylation capacity, especially under physiological stress such as surgery, high inflammation, or low dietary folate. Combined with her SOD2 variant and current surgical recovery, methylation support is especially relevant right now. The MTHFR deficiency, thermolabile type is the primary clinical concern.
Action: methylfolate (not folic acid) + methylcobalamin B12 + P5P B6 · request homocysteine blood test to confirm functional impact
SOD2 — Val16Ala (p.Val16Ala) Homozygous (AA) rs4880 · CADD 13.03
SOD2 (Superoxide Dismutase 2) is the primary antioxidant enzyme inside mitochondria responsible for neutralizing superoxide radicals before they cause cellular damage. The Val16Ala variant impairs transport of SOD2 into the mitochondrial matrix, reducing its activity — meaning oxidative stress inside cells is less efficiently neutralized. Homozygous AA is associated with elevated mitochondrial oxidative stress load, which is particularly significant post-surgery when tissue repair generates substantial reactive oxygen species. Long-term implications include potential contributions to inflammation, cardiovascular risk, and cancer susceptibility (ClinVar: microvascular complications of diabetes 6).
Action: ubiquinol CoQ10 (not ubiquinone) · NAC for glutathione precursor support · Vitamin C + E during recovery · antioxidant-rich diet · prioritize sleep during healing
GSTP1 — Ile105Val Variant present (heterozygous) rs1695 · CADD 0.035
GSTP1 (Glutathione S-Transferase Pi 1) is a phase II liver detoxification enzyme that conjugates toxins, carcinogens, and oxidative byproducts with glutathione for excretion. The Ile105Val variant reduces enzyme efficiency. Combined with her SOD2 burden, this means oxidative byproducts may be cleared more slowly than in someone with both variants wild-type. ClinVar associates this with colorectal neoplasm risk at a population level — this is not diagnostic. The practical concern during surgical recovery is drug metabolism and toxin clearance.
Action: glutathione support (NAC, glycine, selenium) · cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, kale) upregulate GSTP1 · minimize unnecessary medication load during recovery
ADD1 — Gly460Trp Heterozygous (AG) rs4961 · CADD 31
Alpha-Adducin (ADD1) affects sodium-potassium pump activity in kidney tubules, influencing how efficiently the body reabsorbs sodium. The Gly460Trp variant increases sodium reabsorption, creating susceptibility to salt-sensitive hypertension. With a CADD score of 31 (highly pathogenic prediction), this is among the more functionally significant variants in her profile. She does not currently appear hypertensive, but dietary sodium awareness is important preventatively — especially as her cardiovascular system manages surgical recovery stress and vascular manipulation performed by Dr. Roach.
Action: moderate dietary sodium, prioritize potassium-rich foods (bananas, avocado, leafy greens), monitor blood pressure monthly
APOE — e2/e3 (Arg176Cys) Heterozygous (TC) rs7412 · CADD 25.1
APOE e2 is the rarest APOE allele and generally the most cardiovascular-favorable — associated with lower LDL cholesterol and reduced Alzheimer's risk compared to e3/e3 or any e4-carrying genotype. Her e2/e3 heterozygous status is a cardioprotective finding. However, APOE e2 carriers carry a small elevated risk for type III hyperlipoproteinemia (dysbetalipoproteinemia) if dietary saturated fat intake is high — triglycerides and VLDL can accumulate in susceptible individuals. Annual lipid panels are appropriate monitoring.
Favorable overall — annual lipid panel, moderate saturated fat, monitor triglycerides specifically
CFH — His402Tyr Heterozygous (TC) rs1061170
Complement Factor H (CFH) regulates the complement immune pathway responsible for clearing cellular debris from the retina. The His402Tyr variant reduces CFH's ability to bind oxidized lipids accumulating in the macula, allowing inflammatory damage to proceed — the primary driver of age-related macular degeneration (AMD). Heterozygous status is a moderate risk factor (not high-penetrance). AMD typically becomes clinically relevant after age 60. This variant is clinically significant per ClinVar: pathogenic, risk factor for Age-related macular degeneration 4 and Basal laminar drusen (autosomal dominant). Because this is autosomal dominant, heterozygous individuals may manifest the condition rather than just being carriers — monitoring is warranted.
Action: annual dilated eye exam baseline now · lutein + zeaxanthin supplementation (AREDS2 dosing) · avoid smoking absolutely · UV-protective eyewear
Favorable / normal variants
COMT — Val158Met Normal (AA reference allele) rs4680 · CADD 13.15
COMT breaks down dopamine, norepinephrine, and epinephrine in the prefrontal cortex and peripheral tissues. The reference AA genotype at Val158Met is the "fast COMT" phenotype — efficient catecholamine clearance and normal dopamine signaling. She does not carry the slow COMT variant associated with heightened pain sensitivity, stress reactivity, or elevated dopamine tone. This is a favorable finding for surgical pain management, anesthesia response, and stress recovery.
Favorable — standard dopamine/pain response expected, no special considerations for anesthesia recovery
NQO1 — Pro187Ser Normal (GG reference allele) rs1800566 · CADD 24.8
NQO1 (NAD(P)H Quinone Dehydrogenase 1) is a detoxification enzyme that also plays a critical role in recycling CoQ10 from its oxidized to reduced form, and in stabilizing the p53 tumor suppressor protein. The GG reference genotype means she has fully functional NQO1 — no impairment in CoQ10 metabolism from this locus. This means the CoQ10 she takes for SOD2 support will be efficiently recycled and utilized. Contrast with individuals carrying AA at this SNP, who cannot recycle CoQ10 effectively.
Favorable — CoQ10 supplementation will be fully effective and well utilized
LCT/MCM6 — Lactase persistence Homozygous (AA) — persistence variant rs4988235 · CADD 13.46
Homozygous AA at rs4988235 means she carries two copies of the lactase persistence allele — genetically well-equipped to digest dairy throughout adulthood. The lactase enzyme remains active rather than declining post-childhood as it does in most of the world's population. No lactose intolerance concerns from a genetic standpoint. Dairy can be freely included in her diet as a protein and calcium source, which is relevant during SC joint bone healing.
No action needed — dairy consumption appropriate and beneficial during bone recovery
